Eswatini's Sihlangu Clinches Historic COSAFA Cup Victory in Thrilling Final

By **Street Talk Sports Desk** • Published: October 27, 2025

MBABANE – In what is being hailed as the greatest moment in Eswatini football history, Sihlangu defeated the tournament favorites in a penalty shootout last night to lift the COSAFA Cup trophy.

The tension was palpable throughout the match, which remained goalless for 120 minutes of grueling play. The team, led by Captain **Sandile 'Shana' Zwane**, showed incredible resilience, dominating the midfield despite immense pressure from their opponents.

The Road to Victory

Sihlangu’s journey was marked by commitment and tactical discipline. They surprised many analysts by emerging from the group stage undefeated, and then securing a dramatic semi-final win against Lesotho.

  • Group Stage: 3 wins, 0 draws, 0 losses
  • Quarter Finals: Defeated Zambia 1-0
  • Semi Finals: Defeated Lesotho in Extra Time

The final, however, was a masterclass in defense. Goalkeeper **Thabo Maseko** made several stunning saves, keeping the team in contention until the final whistle. The crowd erupted when the winning penalty was scored.

What This Means for Local Sports

The victory is expected to inject significant excitement and investment into Eswatini’s local sports leagues. Analysts predict a surge in youth participation in football and a renewed focus on sports infrastructure across the country.

"This isn't just a trophy; it's a testament to the potential of our nation. Every child who watched that game now knows that, with hard work, we can compete with the best in Africa."
- Minister of Sports and Culture

Celebrations are expected to continue throughout the week, with a national holiday being rumored in honor of the champions.
